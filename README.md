# Telecom Customer Churn Dashboard (Power BI Case Study)

## Overview  

This project presents a **Customer Churn Analytics Dashboard** developed using **Microsoft Power BI**. The dashboard provides an interactive analysis of telecom customer data, focusing on churn behavior, revenue impact, and customer segmentation.

The goal of this project is to transform raw customer and billing data into actionable insights that support **data-driven decision-making**, reduce churn, and maximize revenue retention.

## Context  

Customer churn is a major challenge in the telecom industry, directly impacting revenue, profitability, and long-term growth. Acquiring new customers is significantly more expensive than retaining existing ones, making churn analysis a critical business function.

This dashboard was built to provide a comprehensive view of churn patterns, customer characteristics, and financial impact—allowing stakeholders to identify high-risk segments and implement targeted retention strategies.

---

## Business Questions  

The dashboard aims to answer the following key questions:

- What is the overall churn rate and total number of churned customers?  
- Which contract types and tenure groups have the highest churn rates?  
- How do payment methods and billing preferences influence churn?  
- What customer segments are most likely to churn?  
- What is the revenue loss due to churn, and how much can be recovered?  
- Which services (Internet type, security, etc.) are associated with higher churn?  

---

## Insights  

### Churn Overview  
- Total Customers: **7,043**  
- Churned Customers: **1,869**  
- Overall Churn Rate: **26.5%**  
- Average Monthly Charge: **$64.76**  
- Average Customer Tenure: **32.4 months**  

- Month-to-month contracts have the highest churn (~42%)  
- Customers with 0–12 months tenure show the highest churn (~47%)  
- Fiber optic users churn more compared to DSL and non-internet users  

---

### Customer Profile  

- Senior citizens churn rate: **47.29%**  
- Customers with no partner (**49.7%**) and no dependents (**45%**) churn more  
- Highest risk segment: **Solo seniors**  

- Electronic check users represent the highest churn segment  
- Customers without online security show significantly higher churn (~31%)  

---

### Revenue Impact  

- Monthly Revenue Loss: **$139.13K**  
- Average Revenue per Churned User: **$74.44**  
- Total Revenue at Risk: **$168.98K**  
- Recoverable Revenue Opportunity: **$136K**  

- Month-to-month contracts contribute the largest revenue loss  
- Electronic check payment method leads to the highest churn-related losses  
- Majority of churned revenue comes from fiber optic customers  

---

## Recommendations  

- Encourage customers to switch from month-to-month to long-term contracts through incentives  
- Target high-risk segments (solo seniors, new customers) with personalized retention campaigns  
- Promote secure payment methods and discourage electronic checks  
- Bundle services (fiber + security + support) to increase customer stickiness  
- Offer onboarding programs for customers within the first 12 months  
- Provide loyalty rewards for long-tenure customers  

---

## Possible Impact  

- Reduce churn rate from 26% to below 20%  
- Recover up to **$136K monthly revenue**  
- Improve customer lifetime value (CLV)  
- Increase long-term contract adoption  
- Enable proactive, data-driven retention strategies
